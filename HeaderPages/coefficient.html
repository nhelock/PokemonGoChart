What is an Effective Coefficient?

An Effective Coefficient is a simplified way of understanding statistics of a singular Pokémon, without knowing anything about a second Pokémon. All Stats in Pokémon are related to another Pokémon. A 100 damage move does more damage to a Pokémon with 10 defense than one with 100, for example. In order to get a general baseline on Pokémon effectiveness, I've created two important terms.

Effective Health Coefficient

Normally, the health of a Pokémon is determined simply by a numeric value (like 100). However, a Pokémon with 100 health and 10 defense is not as bulky as a Pokémon with 100 health and 30 defense, obviously. So, this can be simplified simply by multiplying the two together, to understand a total picture on health, and effective health and actual health.

But what's the difference between Actual Health and Effective Health?

Normally, this wouldn't be an important distinction. However, in Max Battles, Pokémon have the ability Max Spirit, which health EVERYONE in the Max Battle based on a percent of the user's HP. For this, effective health doesn't matter, and only actual health matters.
